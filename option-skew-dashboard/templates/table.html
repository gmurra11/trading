Delta 25 Skew

The 25 delta (25âˆ†) skew measures the price of a call option with a delta of 0.25 and the price of a put option that has a delta of 0.25. If the skew increases then puts are becoming more expensive than calls; if the skew decreases, call premiums are going up against puts premiums. A positive value indicates puts are coming more expensive.
Note: T1, T7 and T30 values are based off an average over 1 day, 7 days and 30 days respectively and then compared with current 1D average. T1 is yesterday's 1D file. T7 and T30 have no previous file, rather just an average over 7 / 30 days etc...

Volatility Carry Spread

Positive Carry = Go Short; Positive Carry means the RV is below IV, so this means that IV over estimated the underlying move, the actual move was less. Benefit to net sellers.
Negative Carry = Go Long; Negative Carry means RV is above IV, so exceeded the implied volatility, better for net buyers (ie: you want more volatility).

Spread is the difference between IV and RV (Realized Volatility).
